What Hath God Wrought?

What Hath God Wrought? is a series of small games where you play the role of a 19th century telegraph operator.

Each game uses a physical telegraph key connected to a computer (the most recent iteration of the hardware does not have the attached NES controller featured in the photo above). Players tap out actual morse code (or, in some cases, a more simplified pidgin code). The games all share a visual style, made up of found woodcut illustrations from the 19th century.

I've largely been using the project to explore the use of props as narrative engagement, and how we can use physical controllers to help encourage role-playing.

The current list of games includes:

  • A narrative IF game where you befriend the other telegraph operators on your line, exploring the fascinating tight-knit culture of telegraph operators.

  • A twitchy arcade typing game where you help gamblers run a horseracing scam. The instant a race ends, tell your partners in crime the next town over who won before the official results are delivered by train!

  • A memory-based game that teaches you a series of rules ("when you receive this series of dits and dahs, respond this way") and slowly ratchets up the speed and number of rules as you perform better. It's sort of like the iOS game Rules! crossed with Simon Says.

Differences in the final design

The custom hardware I currently have is great, but I'm working on wiring up a vintage 19th century telegraph key and sounder (photo attached). The main difference here is the addition of a sounder (that big cube), which will let the game have mechanically-produced audio output; it uses magnets to make two metal bars loudly clack against each other.

If things go very well, I'll have a custom-built sounder by GDC, rather than the vintage hardware. If not, I can show the game either with my current custom controller (sans sounder) or with the vintage controller (with working sounder).

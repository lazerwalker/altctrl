What Hath God Wrought?

What Hath God Wrought? is a series of small games about the telegraph and its cultural and sociological impact in the 19th century.

It will be featured in the ALT.CTRL.GDC exhibition at GDC 2015 on March 2-6, 2015.

Each game puts you in the shoes of a telegraph operator in the 19th century. Each is controlled via a physical telegraph key, and features graphics made up of found woodcut illustrations from the 19th century.

I've largely been using the project to explore the use of props as narrative engagement, and how we can use physical controllers to help encourage role-playing.

The current list of games includes:

  • A narrative IF game where you befriend the other telegraph operators on your line, exploring the fascinating tight-knit culture of telegraph operators.

  • A twitchy arcade typing game where you help gamblers run a horseracing scam. The instant a race ends, tell your partners in crime the next town over who won before the official results are delivered by train!

  • A memory-based game that teaches you a series of rules ("when you receive this series of dits and dahs, respond this way") and slowly ratchets up the speed and number of rules as you perform better. It's sort of like the iOS game Rules! crossed with Simon Says.
